Chapter 3 Designing your website

This chapter provides some tips and tricks for designing your website to reflect personal style and achieve your goals. Designing your website is your opportunity to be creative and artistic, but it can be a major pitfall if you’re not careful. The key is to select a Hugo theme (more on this later) that is 90% or more of what you want your website to look and feel like out of the box. The remaining 10% of your design can come from basic customizations such as colors, fonts, and simple tweaks to the HTML. If you customize too much, you may find yourself constantly fighting to compensate for any poor design choices you may have made.

Note that website design is highly personal and subjective. There are no rules or requirements. My goal with this chapter is to steer you towards some designs that I have found to be attractive, flexible, and responsive, and to also empower you to discover new designs that are better suited to you and your goals.

3.1 Selecting a Hugo theme

The look and feel of your website is largely determined by your choice of Hugo themes. Once you find a theme you would like to try, you can specify this theme when you create a new blogdown website.

3.1.1 Recommended themes

Depending on your goals, you may be more or less restricted to certain themes. For example, if you intend to create content (e.g. posts) from R Markdown documents that feature code, tables, and visualizations, then you may find that certain themes do a poor job of rendering these types of output. On the other hand, if you intend to create content using basic markdown, you may not experience any content rendering issues with any of the themes you consider. Either way, you’ll likely go through a few rounds of trial and error before you settle on a theme that meets all of your needs.

Here are some themes that I recommend based on performance, content rendering, and personal style preference.

Recommended themes for content written in R Markdown

  • Cupper - Simple, clean, readable, minimalist. Great for portfolios and documentation.
  • Tranquilpeak - Modern, clean layout. Easy to customize and very readable. Great for personal websites, blogs, and portfolios.
  • Eureka - Clean, professional layout. Easy to customize. Great for portfolios and professional landing pages.

3.1.2 Creating a website with your chosen theme

To create a new blogdown website with a new Hugo theme, follow these steps:

  1. Browse the list of Hugo themes. Click on a theme you like, then click “Demo” to explore a complete example.

  2. Once you find something you really like, click on “Download” to be taken to the source code in GitHub. Example

  3. To use the theme in your website, copy the user/theme-name in the GitHub URL. For example, if the source code is located at https://github.com/devcows/hugo-universal-theme, you’ll want to copy devcows/hugo-universal-theme.

  4. Follow the steps to create a new blogdown website. This time, configure the website to use your chosen theme instead of the default theme. For example, replace yihui/hugo-lithium with devcows/hugo-universal-theme to use your chosen theme.
